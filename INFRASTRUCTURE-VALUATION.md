## üìã WHAT HAS BEEN BUILT: COMPREHENSIVE DESCRIPTION

### Third-Person Narrative

The development team behind Unykorn Layer 1 has constructed a **comprehensive institutional-grade blockchain infrastructure** designed specifically for regulated financial markets. Over an estimated 18-24 month development period, the team has built what represents one of the most complete enterprise blockchain platforms in the institutional finance space.

### Scale and Scope

The platform consists of **over 205 custom smart contracts** totaling approximately **75,000+ lines of Solidity code**, making it one of the larger blockchain infrastructure projects developed for institutional use. The codebase successfully compiles into **286 Solidity files** (including dependencies) and generates **926 TypeScript type definitions**, providing comprehensive SDK coverage for developers.

### Technical Architecture

At its foundation, the team has implemented a **permissioned EVM-compatible Layer 1 blockchain** based on Hyperledger Besu, featuring IBFT 2.0/QBFT consensus mechanisms capable of achieving sub-2-second block finality. The network has been designed to support **21 validators initially**, with architecture allowing expansion to **100+ validators** for geographical distribution and increased decentralization.

The smart contract architecture follows **industry best practices** with:
- **UUPS proxy patterns** for upgradeability across all major systems
- **Comprehensive role-based access control** (RBAC) on every contract
- **Reentrancy protection** throughout critical financial operations
- **Circuit breaker mechanisms** for emergency response
- **Pausable functionality** for risk management

### Regulatory Compliance Framework

The team has built an **exceptionally comprehensive regulatory compliance infrastructure** that sets the platform apart from competitors:

**Basel III/IV Implementation:**
The system includes sophisticated capital adequacy ratio (CAR) monitoring, risk-weighted asset (RWA) calculations, eligible reserve tracking, and liability monitoring. This positions the platform to serve regulated financial institutions requiring compliance with international banking standards.

**ISO 20022 Integration:**
Full support for ISO 20022 payment message standards has been implemented, including generation and parsing of pacs.009 (Financial Institution Credit Transfer), camt.053 (Bank to Customer Statement), pacs.008 (Customer Credit Transfer), and pain.001 (Customer Credit Transfer Initiation) messages. This enables seamless integration with traditional financial infrastructure.

**MiCA Compliance:**
The platform implements the European Union's Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, including specific support for E-Money Tokens (EMT) and Asset-Referenced Tokens (ART), positioning it for European market entry.

**KYC/AML Framework:**
A comprehensive know-your-customer (KYC) and anti-money laundering (AML) registry system has been built, featuring jurisdictional policy engines, professional investor verification, geographic restriction enforcement, sanctions screening integration, and FATF Travel Rule compliance.

### Multi-Rail Settlement Infrastructure

One of the platform's most impressive achievements is its **multi-rail settlement system**, which integrates with **10+ different settlement networks**:

**Traditional Finance Integration:**
- SWIFT GPI adapter for real-time payment tracking and gpi Universal Confirmations
- Bank settlement system connectors
- Custodian reporting mechanisms

**Blockchain Settlement Rails:**
- Chainlink CCIP for cross-chain messaging
- Circle's CCTP (Cross-Chain Transfer Protocol) for USDC settlements
- Custom ERC20 and native token rails

**Institutional Infrastructure:**
- BIS Project Agor√° integration for tokenized wholesale deposit settlements
- RLN Multi-CBDC Bridge for central bank digital currency interoperability
- Fnality Payment System integration for institutional payment settlements
- Midnight blockchain integration for privacy-preserving transactions using zero-knowledge proofs

**Settlement Patterns:**
The team has implemented multiple sophisticated settlement mechanisms including two-phase commit (2PC), delivery versus payment (DvP), payment versus payment (PvP), multilateral netting, and milestone-based escrow. This provides institutional clients with familiar settlement patterns adapted for blockchain technology.

### Stablecoin Infrastructure Suite

The platform features **four distinct stablecoin implementations**, each serving different market needs:

1. **Fiat-Custodial Stablecoin:** Bank-backed with 1:1 fiat reserves, featuring upgradeable architecture and ERC20Permit support for gasless transactions.

2. **Crypto-Collateralized Stablecoin:** Over-collateralized model with automated liquidation mechanisms and oracle-fed pricing.

3. **Asset-Referenced Token (ART):** MiCA-compliant stablecoin backed by a basket of assets, featuring NAV-based rebase functionality for yield distribution.

4. **Multi-Issuer Stablecoin:** Allows multiple financial institutions to issue the same stablecoin with shared liquidity but institution-specific compliance rules.

All stablecoins feature **KYC-gated transfers**, **compliance policy enforcement**, **proof-of-reserves validation**, and **ISO 20022 event emission** for regulatory reporting.

### CBDC Infrastructure

Recognizing the growing importance of central bank digital currencies, the team has built comprehensive **CBDC infrastructure** including:
- Core CBDC issuance and management smart contracts
- Tiered wallet system separating retail and wholesale access
- Cross-border CBDC bridge for international settlements
- Programmable monetary policy engines
- Central bank control mechanisms for supply management

This positions the platform to serve central banks exploring CBDC implementation.

### Real-World Asset (RWA) Tokenization

The team has developed **extensive RWA tokenization infrastructure** supporting **8+ asset classes**:
